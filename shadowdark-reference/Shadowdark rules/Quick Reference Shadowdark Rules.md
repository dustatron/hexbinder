
# Shadowdark RPG Quick Reference

## Core Mechanics

**d20 Roll-Over System**: d20 + ability modifier + bonuses vs DC
- **Advantage/Disadvantage**: Roll 2d20, take highest/lowest
- **Ability Modifiers**: STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA (-5 to +5)

### Difficulty Classes
- **Easy**: DC 9
- **Normal**: DC 12
- **Hard**: DC 15
- **Extreme**: DC 18

### When to Roll
- When an action has negative consequences
- When the action requires skill
- When there is time pressure

---

## Movement & Exploration

### Distance Categories
- **Close**: 5 feet
- **Near**: Within 30 feet
- **Far**: Beyond 30 feet, but within sight

### Movement Actions
**Climbing**: STR or DEX check, climb half speed. Fall if you fail by 5+ points.

**Swimming**: Swim half speed (STR check in rough water). CON check each round you hold your breath.

**Moving Through**: Move freely through allies. STR or DEX to move through enemies.

**Falling**: 1d6 damage per 10 feet fallen.

### Stealth
- **Hide**: DEX check to go undetected
- Can't hide while creatures can see you
- Impossible to hide with nowhere to stay out of sight
- **Detecting**: Looking in the right place automatically reveals hidden creatures
- Otherwise, WIS check to perceive sneaking/hiding creatures

---

## Combat

### Initiative
Roll d20 + DEX modifier each round. Winner starts, then play continues **clockwise**.

### Attack Rolls
- **Melee**: d20 + STR + weapon bonus vs AC
- **Ranged**: d20 + DEX + weapon bonus vs AC
- **Damage**: Weapon die + STR (melee) or DEX (ranged/finesse)

### Critical Hits & Fumbles
- **Critical Hit** (Natural 20): Double the damage roll results
- **Fumble** (Natural 1): GM's discretion for consequences

### Surprise
Creatures have **advantage** on attack rolls against surprised targets.

### Terrain
- **Partial Cover**: Attacker has disadvantage
- **Full Cover**: Can't attack what is blocked
- **Rough Terrain**: Move half normal distance

### Morale Checks
When enemies are at half health or half numbers:
- **DC 15 WIS check** or flee

### Death & Dying
- **0 HP = Dying**: Roll d4 + CON for rounds until death
- **Natural 20**: Rise with 1 HP
- **Stabilize**: DC 15 INT check, target stops dying

### Knockout
Choose to knockout instead of killing when reducing enemy to 0 HP.

## Spellcasting

### Casting a Spell
- **Wizards**: Roll d20 + INT vs Spell DC
- **Priests**: Roll d20 + WIS vs Spell DC
- **Spell DC**: 10 + Spell Tier

### Spell Failure
Spell does not take effect. Can't cast again until after a complete rest.

### Critical Success (Natural 20)
Double one of the spell's numerical effects.

### Critical Failure (Natural 1)
**Wizard**: Can't cast again until after rest. Roll on Mishap Table (pg. 46).

**Priest**: Can't cast that spell again until penance to deity and successful rest.

### Spell Focus
- **Success**: Spell stays in effect until start of your next turn
- **Failure**: Spell ends (but you don't lose the spell)
- **Distraction**: Immediately make spellcasting check to maintain focus

### Scrolls & Wands
**Scrolls**:
- DC 10 + Spell Tier
- Failing does not impact known spells
- One-time use
- Mishap on critical failure

**Wands**:
- Failed attempt: wand stops working until rest
- Critical failure: wand breaks and causes Mishap

---

## Penance (for Priests)

Requires holy quest, ritualistic atonement, or material sacrifice:
- **Tier 1**: 5 gp
- **Tier 2**: 20 gp
- **Tier 3**: 40 gp
- **Tier 4**: 90 gp
- **Tier 5**: 150 gp

*Inadequate penance makes spell loss permanent.*

---

## Resting

**Requirements**: Consume a ration and sleep for 8 hours

### Rest Benefits
- Regain all lost HP
- Recover stat damage
- Regain talents, spells, or item abilities

### Interruption
DC 12 CON check. On failure, consume ration but gain no benefit.

### Campfire
Combine 3 torches into a campfire. Lasts up to 8 hours while attended. Casts light to near distance.

## Random Encounters & Environment

### Check Frequency by Danger Level
- **Unsafe**: Check every 3 rounds (dungeon) / 3 hours (overland)
- **Risky**: Check every 2 rounds (dungeon) / 2 hours (overland)
- **Deadly**: Check every round (dungeon) / 1 hour (overland)

## Light & Darkness

### Total Darkness
- Creatures not darkness-adapted have **disadvantage** on sight-based tasks
- Environment becomes **deadly**
- Make random encounter checks **every round**

### Light Sources
Determine remaining time on light source: **1d6 × 10 minutes**

---

## Overland Travel

### Travel Pace
Travel up to 8 hours per day. Must pass increasing CON checks to push further.
- **Hexes**: 6 miles across

### Navigation
In unfamiliar territory, navigator makes INT check upon exiting hex.
- **Failure**: Group moves into random adjacent hex

### Food & Water
Go 3 days without consuming a ration.
- Take **1 CON damage** each day after (death at 0)
- May forage for 1 ration per day with **INT check**

---

## Traps & Hazards

### Traps
**Searching**: Searching specific area/object automatically reveals trap.

**Disabling**: Thieves and tinkering-trained characters can disable traps.
- Enough time + reasonable method = success
- Time pressure or high skill = stat check required

**Design Note**: Traps should have a hint or tell.

### Hazards
- **Movement Restriction**: Quicksand, slippery ice
- **Damage**: Toxic spores, acid rain (ongoing damage)
- **Weakening**: Antimagic zones, strength-sapping vapors

---

## Luck Tokens

- Each player can only have **1 at a time**
- Cash in to **re-roll** any roll just made
- Can be given to another player

## Character Classes Summary

### Fighter
- **HD**: d8
- **Equipment**: All weapons and armor
- **Special**: Multiple attacks, weapon mastery

### Priest
- **HD**: d6
- **Equipment**: All weapons and armor
- **Special**: Divine spells, turn undead

### Thief
- **HD**: d4
- **Equipment**: Limited armor
- **Special**: Backstab, thief skills (advantage on specific checks)

### Wizard
- **HD**: d4
- **Equipment**: No armor
- **Special**: Arcane spells, learn spells from scrolls

---

## XP & Advancement

**XP for Gold**: 1 XP per 1 gp of treasure retrieved to safety

**Carousing**: Spend gold between sessions for XP
- 1 gp = 1 XP
- Roll for consequences
